A graph of the brain using diffusion MRI data

Introduction

This is an example of a visualization of a network of connections. It demonstrates some of the advantages of an interactive visualization for complex data over a simple image. You will notice on the figure that indeed the regions of the brain are very well connected to each other. Although interesting, it can be difficult for a non-expert to find information on this graph. Among the interesting thing to notice:

  • regions within the hemispheres are more connected than the ones across hemispheres,
  • the subcortical regions seem to be important providers of connections across hemispheres,
  • the is no region that connect to all the other ones,
  • the Insula is connected to all the regions of its hemisphere,

The size of the nodes is related to the degree (number of connections). The reader can immediately spot the central regions, connected to many parts of the brain.

Additional links are provided in the following, to filtered data, with less links. Pattern can be more easily spotted.

Context

The diffusion MRI technique is a mean to probe the quantity of neuronal fibers between different regions of the brain. These fibers, located in the white matter, are bundles of thousands of axions and are the "communication highways" between brain regions.

In the following figure, each node is a brain region of the Desikan-Killiany Atlas. Each vertex illustrates the connection between two brain regions. The node haven't been placed according to their spatial coordinates on the brain. They are free to move, providing they follow some physical rules given by the force layout.

Visualization

One can drag nodes inside the application and see the recombination of the graph. In addition, putting the mouse over a node will highlight its connections.
